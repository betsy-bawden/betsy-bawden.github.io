Introduction

The Sagrada Familia is one of the most well-known landmarks in Spain. It is located in the city of Barcelona. It is a Roman Catholic Church designed by the famous Catalan architect, Antoni Gaudi. What makes it very notable is that while construction started in 1882, it has yet to be completed.

History

Contruction for the Sagrada Familia started in 1882, under the architect Francisco de Paula del Villar. When he resigned, Antoni Gaudi took over. Gaudi's transformation of the original design was significant because he combined both gothic and art noveau designs. He worked on the project for the remainder of his life, until he died in an accident at the age of 73. He was so devoted to the project that his body is actually buried in the crypts. At the time of his death, the project was less than a quarter of the way complete.

Present Day

Contruction continues to this day and reached the halfway mark in 2010. It is anticipated that contruction will be finished by 2026, the 100 year anniversary of Gaudi's death. Modern day technologies have helped speed up the process, although many of the most challenging parts of the design have yet to be started. The monument was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and in 2010, Pope Benedict XVI consecrated it and proclaimed it a minor basilica.

Controversy

The monument has been a topic of debate among much of the population of Barcelona. Some of the biggest concerns have been:

  • If it will compete with Barcelona's main Cathedral
  • That the work after Gaudi's death did not stick to his design
