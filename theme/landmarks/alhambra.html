About

The Alhambra is a palace located in Granada, Spain. It was originally constructed as a small fortress around the year AD 889, but it was renovated and rebuilt in the mid-13th century. While the palace has spent centuries being controlled by both Christian and Muslim rulers, many of the design influences (such as the intricate shapes that decorate the palace) are representative of Islam.

Generalife

One of the buildings that surrounds the palace is known as Generalife. When the area was ruled by the Nasrids, Generalife served as their summer palace. Thus, it is very green and has large gardens and fountains.

Present Day

Now, the Alhamhra is one of the biggest tourist attractions in Spain (and the world) and is visited by millions of people every year. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and has served as inspiration for countless songs, movies, and other artworks.
