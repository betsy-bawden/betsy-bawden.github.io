About

The official language of Spain is Spanish. It is the most spoken language, and is spoken by almost 99% of the population as a first or second language. The variety of Spanish spoken in Spain is known as Castilian or European Spanish. However, there are many variants of the language and certain features vary from region to region.

Some regional dialects of Spanish are:

  • Andalusian Spanish
  • Canarian Spanish
  • Castrapo (spoken in Galicia)
  • Murcian Spanish

While there are several varieties and accents, they are mutually intelligible and can be understood all across the country.

History

Around 2000 BC, the inhabitants of the Iberian peninsula spoke celtic languages. However, they began learning Latin from the Romans. The combination of celtic languages and Latin resulted in what is now known as Vulgar Latin.

Latin remained the predominant language of the region for many years until the arrival of the Moors. It can now be estimated that roughly 3000-4000 words in Spanish have Arabic origins/roots.

The Castilian dialect of Spanish began to dominate the region around the 13th century, thanks to King Alfonso X. The influence of Castilian Spanish continued to grow as Catholic Kingdoms took over the peninsula, and once Queen Isabella and King Ferdinand declared it the official language of the kingdom.
