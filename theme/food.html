Tapas

The term "Tapa" doesn't refer to a specific dish. It is a variety of small plates that can be eaten individually or combined to make a full meal. Some examples of tapas are ham and cheese, small sandwiches, or Spanish tortillas. Each restaurant prepares tapas a little differently, and while some let you order a specific tapa, others will bring them to your table without your input.


In the region of Andalucia, tapas are free, granted that you keep ordering drinks. This is unique to the south of Spain, and it extremely common in the city of Granada. All you have to do is sit down at a restaurant and order a drink. When the waiter or waitress brings you your drink, they'll also bring a tapa that you ordre of a tapa of their choice. When you're done, you simply order another drink, and they'll bring you another tapa.

Paella

Paella is a Valencian dish that is common throughout the south of Spain. There are many varieties of paella, although the original Valencian form consists of rice, beans, meat (usually chicken, rabbit, or duck), beans, and seasonings such as rosemary and saffron. Other varieties include seafood paella and veggie paella.


Churros

Churros are fried pastries common throughout Spain and Portugal. They are commonly eaten for breakfast, and can be served with chocolate, dulce de leche, or coffee.


Empanadas

Empanadas are a pastry common throughout both Spain and the Americas. They are prepared by folding dough over a stuffing or filling, and then frying or baking them. They can be either savory or sweet.

Empanadas originate from Galicia, in northwestern Spain. Now, countries throughout Latin America have their own variations of the dish, although the principle remains the same everywhere.
